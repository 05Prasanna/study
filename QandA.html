Questions and Answers for "The Letter"

What is the main theme of "The Letter"?

The main themes explored in "The Letter" are morality, sacrifice, and the consequences of one's actions.

Who are the main characters in "The Letter"?

The main characters are Leslie and Doris, a couple living in Malaya.

What is the setting of "The Letter"?

The story is set in Malaya (now Malaysia), where Leslie and Doris reside.

What event sets the story in motion?

The story begins with Leslie and Doris hosting a dinner party for their friends, during which a deadly cobra enters the room and causes chaos.

What dilemma does Doris face when Leslie is severely injured?

Doris faces the dilemma of whether to send a letter to the district officer requesting urgent medical assistance for Leslie. However, she knows that sending the letter would reveal the illegal activities of the party guests, which could have serious consequences for everyone involved.

What does Doris ultimately decide to do?

Despite her reservations, Doris decides to send the letter, believing that saving her husband's life is the top priority.

How does the story end?

The story's resolution is not explicitly stated in the provided text, but it suggests that Doris's decision to send the letter reflects her belief in prioritizing her husband's life over the potential consequences of revealing the illegal activities.
